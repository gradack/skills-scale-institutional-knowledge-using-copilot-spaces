# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Project Sponsors

### Role Summary
Project Sponsors provide strategic direction and executive-level support for projects. They ensure alignment with organizational goals, secure necessary resources, and resolve high-level roadblocks.

### Responsibilities
- Provide strategic vision and ensure project aligns with business objectives
- Secure budget, resources, and organizational commitment
- Remove escalated roadblocks and organizational barriers
- Make executive-level decisions on scope and priority changes
- Champion the project at leadership levels
- Review and approve major milestones and deliverables

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver strategic business value
- Maintain organizational alignment and support
- Enable teams to succeed by removing impediments

### Typical Communication
- Monthly project status reviews with Project Manager
- Executive briefings on major milestones and risks
- Escalation resolution meetings as needed
- Strategic decision meetings with Product and Project leads

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Receives status updates, resolves escalations, provides strategic guidance
- **With Product Managers**: Aligns on product vision and business priorities
- **With Stakeholders**: Communicates organizational strategy and secures buy-in

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They gather, analyze, and document requirements, ensuring that development teams understand what needs to be built and why.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into clear, actionable user stories and acceptance criteria
- Facilitate requirements workshops and clarification sessions
- Analyze processes and identify opportunities for improvement
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business requirements
- Maintain requirements traceability throughout the project

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions address actual business needs
- Reduce ambiguity and rework through clear requirements
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Backlog refinement sessions with Product and Project teams
- User story walkthroughs with developers
- Acceptance validation sessions with QA and stakeholders

### Key Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Collaborates on backlog prioritization and requirement refinement
- **With Developers**: Clarifies requirements and acceptance criteria during implementation
- **With QA Lead**: Ensures test cases align with acceptance criteria
- **With Stakeholders**: Gathers requirements and validates solutions

---

## QA Leads

### Role Summary
QA Leads coordinate all quality assurance activities, establish testing standards, and ensure that delivered software meets quality expectations and acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain quality standards and test strategies
- Coordinate testing activities across the team
- Plan test coverage for features and releases
- Manage defect tracking and resolution workflows
- Ensure test automation is properly implemented
- Report on quality metrics and testing progress
- Participate in planning to estimate testing effort

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, defect-free software
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage
- Reduce post-release defects through thorough testing

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning sessions
- Test plan reviews with Project and Product teams
- Defect triage meetings with developers
- Quality status reports in stakeholder updates

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Collaborates on testability, reviews test results, manages defect resolution
- **With Business Analysts**: Validates that test cases cover all acceptance criteria
- **With Project Managers**: Reports testing progress, risks, and resource needs
- **With Product Managers**: Clarifies quality expectations and acceptance criteria

---

## Change Managers

### Role Summary
Change Managers oversee the change control process, ensuring that proposed changes are properly evaluated, approved, and communicated. They manage stakeholder communications and help teams navigate organizational change.

### Responsibilities
- Manage the change control board and approval workflows
- Evaluate change requests for impact, risk, and dependencies
- Coordinate stakeholder communication about changes
- Document change decisions and rationale
- Ensure changes follow established governance processes
- Track change metrics and lessons learned
- Facilitate change communication and training when needed

### Goals
- Maintain project stability through controlled change management
- Ensure stakeholders understand and support changes
- Minimize disruption from necessary changes

### Typical Communication
- Change control board meetings
- Change impact assessments with technical and business teams
- Stakeholder notifications and change communications
- Change status reports in project updates

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinates change request evaluation and approval processes
- **With Product Managers**: Assesses business impact of proposed changes
- **With All Team Members**: Receives change requests and communicates approved changes
- **With Stakeholders**: Manages expectations and communicates change impacts

---

## General Stakeholders

### Role Summary
General Stakeholders represent any internal or external parties who have an interest in or are affected by the project. They provide input, requirements, and feedback throughout the project lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements and priorities from their perspective
- Participate in requirements gathering and validation sessions
- Review and provide feedback on deliverables
- Represent the needs of their user group or organization
- Attend demos and milestone reviews
- Approve deliverables within their area of responsibility

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers value to their area or user group
- Maintain visibility into project progress
- Influence priorities to address their needs

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder interviews and requirements sessions
- Monthly project status updates
- Demo and milestone review meetings
- Feedback sessions on prototypes and deliverables

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Managers**: Receives status updates and provides feedback on progress
- **With Product Managers**: Shares requirements and priorities from their perspective
- **With Business Analysts**: Provides detailed requirements and validates solutions
- **With Project Sponsor**: Escalates concerns and aligns on strategic priorities

## Role Interaction Map

Understanding how these personas interact is critical for effective project execution. Below is a summary of key collaboration patterns:

### Planning Phase
- **Project Sponsor** approves project charter and resources
- **Project Manager** coordinates planning activities
- **Product Manager** defines product vision and priorities
- **Business Analyst** gathers and documents requirements
- **Stakeholders** provide input on requirements
- **QA Lead** participates in planning to assess quality requirements and testing effort

### Execution Phase
- **Developers** implement features
- **Business Analyst** clarifies requirements as questions arise
- **QA Lead** coordinates testing activities
- **Project Manager** tracks progress and manages risks
- **Change Manager** evaluates and approves change requests
- **Product Manager** makes prioritization decisions

### Review and Release Phase
- **QA Lead** validates quality and acceptance criteria
- **Stakeholders** participate in demos and provide feedback
- **Product Manager** approves release readiness
- **Project Manager** coordinates release activities
- **Change Manager** communicates changes to affected parties
- **Project Sponsor** reviews milestone completion

### Escalation Path
When issues arise that cannot be resolved at the team level:
1. **Team Member** → **Project Manager**
2. **Project Manager** → **Product Manager** or **Project Sponsor**
3. **Project Sponsor** → **Executive Leadership** (for organizational barriers)
